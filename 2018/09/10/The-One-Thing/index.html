The One Thing

Gary Keller, Jay Papasan
10-09-2018
motivation, self-dev

Brief summary

In his book Gary Keller explains how focusing on one task is the most efficient way to get things done. He details on why having multiple things to do at one will make anyone less performant and more distracted. He also demystifies the belief of multi-tasking. If we turn our daily to-do list into success list, we would be more productive over time.
He emphasize on the way we should attack big problems, step-by-step.

Keypoints

1 The one thing

  • Focus on one thing at a time.
  • Multitasking is a myth.
  • Extraordinary results are directly determined by how narrow you can make your focus.

2 The domino effect

  • The domino effect, small daily changes lead to huge long-term effects.
    • Find our lead domino and make it falls.

3 Success leaves clues

  • No one succeed alone.

4 Everything matters equally

  • Activity is unrelated to productivity.
  • To-do lists are survival lists.
  • Success lists are for achievers.
  • We should use the extreme pareto. 1/99% over 20/80%
  • Postpone everything which is not in the 1%.
  • Checking-off is not productivity.
